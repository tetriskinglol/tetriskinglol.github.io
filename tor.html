What is Tor?

Tor (The Onion Router) is a privacy-focused network that allows users to browse the internet anonymously. It works by routing your connection through multiple volunteer-run servers (nodes), hiding your real IP address.

How the Tor Network Works

Tor uses a technique called onion routing:

  • Your data is encrypted multiple times
  • It passes through 3 random nodes (entry, relay, exit)
  • Each node only knows the previous and next step

This makes tracking the original user extremely difficult.

Key Features

  • Anonymity and privacy
  • Access to .onion websites
  • Bypasses censorship
  • Decentralized network

Risks & Limitations

  • Slower internet speeds
  • Exit nodes can see unencrypted traffic
  • Used by both privacy advocates and criminals

⚠️ Legal Notice

The Tor network is a privacy tool designed to help protect anonymity online. It is commonly used for legitimate purposes such as protecting personal privacy and accessing restricted information.

However, using Tor to access or purchase illegal goods or services is against the law. Engaging in illegal activity on the dark web can result in serious legal consequences.

This page is for educational purposes only. Always follow the law and use tools responsibly.
